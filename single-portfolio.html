What is heart surgery?

Heart surgery can correct certain heart problems when other treatments haven't worked or can't be used. In some cases, heart surgery may be a medical emergency. For example, surgery for a severe heart attack may need to be done right away. In other cases, you can plan heart surgery ahead of time. Some heart surgeries are major operations, such as heart bypass surgery for blocked arteries in the heart. Other surgeries are more minor heart procedures, such as putting in a pacemaker.

Heart surgery conditions:

There are different types of heart surgery that can help treat many heart conditions:

  • Coronary artery disease (CAD) - when a sticky substance called plaque narrows or blocks the arteries that supply blood to your heart muscle.
  • Heart valve disease – problems with the valves that control the flow of blood through your heart.
  • Arrhythmia (CAD) - problems with your heart rate or rhythm. They are caused by changes in the electrical signals that control your heart rate.
  • Heart failure - when a sticky substance called plaque narrows or blocks the arteries that supply blood to your heart muscle.

The information on this site should not be used as a substitute for professional medical care or advice. Contact a health care provider if you have questions about your health. Heart surgery is an operation to fix or improve how your heart works. It can correct problems you were born with or problems you develop over time. These can range from structural or heart rhythm problems to blockages. The type of surgery you have depends on your problem.

What are the risks of heart surgery?

Like all surgery, heart surgery has risks even though the results are often excellent. The risks include:

  • Bleeding
  • Infection, fever, and swelling
  • A problem from anesthesia (medicine that makes you sleep during surgery)
  • Arrhythmia
  • Damage to the heart, kidneys, liver, and lungs
  • Stroke
  • The surgery is an emergency.

How to do heart surgery?

The approach a surgeon uses to do heart surgery depends on your heart problem, your general health, and other factors :

  • Open-heart surgery:is when the surgeon cuts the chest open to reach the heart.
  • Heart valve disease Off-pump heart surgery: Open-heart surgery on a beating heart without using a heart-lung bypass machine.
  • Arrhythmia (CAD) Minimally invasive heart surgery: It uses small cuts between the ribs. The cuts may be as small as 2 to 3 inches.
  • Heart failure Robotic-assisted surgery: It is a type of minimally invasive surgery. The surgeon uses a computer to control tools.
